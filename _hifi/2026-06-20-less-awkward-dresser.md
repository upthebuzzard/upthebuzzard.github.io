Suddenly, the awkward alcove was no more. A furniture shuffle saw to that. We now had a nice oak dresser facing the room - a much better orientation for hifi. The looks of the thing became the priority. Less "it'll have to go there" and more "it'll have to look nice there". 

The Elac Debut B5.2 speakers, while sounding even better now, filling the room nicely when (at last) pointing into the room were, sadly, not the best lookers. Not actually huge, but still too big, too black and speaker-y, and not oak brown-y. 

By a nice coincidence, in the office I had recently upgraded from Dali Zensor 1 speakers to Dali Oberon 1 (oak+white, yes second-hand), and *they* looked much more oak dresser-appropriate. Offered into place, they sounded great, perhaps better, albeit not as room-filling as the Elacs. But looks-wise, no contest. The Dalis were here to stay. 

A step back in the office, alas. Had just got used to the improvement from Zensor to Oberon. Irked a bit. But only a bit. The Zensor 1s were still fine.

And there it might have ended, but we were back to a previous issue where the record player picked up a hum from the CD player if it was too close above it. The new dresser arrangement forced all the components into a clumsy stack, and it looked messy (as well as humming).

Found a small, square, mid-height Ikea table which fitted the gap perfectly, within 1cm. It was fate. Kitted it out with shelving to separate the separates. Slid them all into place. Except, ugh, the amp would not slide in without major faffage. Missed this when measuring up. The inside gap between the table legs was too narrow. Only worked if all other pieces were removed, and the amp angled in, then everything else stacked back in. Very clumsy. A total pain if any cabling needing adjusting.

An opportunity for a series of GAS attacks...

1. A narrower amp, and why not get one with a decent DAC (to replace the separate DAC)? Much internetting later and a reduced Mission 778X was purchased. 

2. And the CD player? Old, nearly full width. Not really getting with the new half-width vibe we now had going. More internetting, and really very little money later, an SMSL PL100 CD Player. Remembered just in time that the (not supplied) USB power adaptor had to be above a particular minimum power output. UX is not as good, but sounds great.

3. The uninformed might say 'coincidentally', but the amp had a sub out, *and* there was a bit of spare room down beside the dresser. So, a Cambridge Audio X201 subwoofer (second-hand) later, we had ourselves quite a setup.


And yes, it all sounds epic. Volume levels can be cranked up safely without booming up in the alcove. Looks nice. No hum. All usable. A most excellent series of changes.
